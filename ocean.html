My MSci research at Goldsmiths¬†explored how people collaborative make music remotely. I researched how different technical and experience design approaches can enable successful web-based remote music making.

I created a series of prototypes and workshops & found that roles naturally emerge when people jam together remotely and people have more fun when creating together.

Ocean has been iterated over the last few years since graduating as a beloved side project. I collaborate with two friends (who work in design & music) to design new features and market the platform.

Who Why How üë©‚ÄçüöÄ

Ocean is aimed at people who are just starting out making music. Our primary research found that beginners find DAWs overwhelming & some people are locked out by the high price tags they often come with. We also understand that short, fast self-expression is the fundamental way young people express themselves online.

Taking what we know about the User, we can leverage the magic of modern Browsers & web apps. The Web Audio API provide a solid base to explore really cool Audio projects on the web & tone.js helps get things moving quickly. This new lease of creative life on the web creates a perfect storm for projects like Ocean.

The aim of Ocean is to build an accessible simple way to make music and break down the barriers associated with electronic music production.

Design üïπ

Fun, Fast & Simple are Ocean's core design principles. We focus on emoji & minimal text to allow Ocean to be used by as many people as possible. Otherwise complex digital music concepts, such as time stretching, are boiled down to simple and fun controls.

Built for collaboration

The main session view is straight to business, focusing on the main sequencer. Friends are added to the session simply by sharing the project URL and all changes to the session are synced and saved in real time.

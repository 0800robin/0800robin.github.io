The Mission

Peter 2.0 was, by some distance, the most extraordinary project I‚Äôve ever had the privilege to contribute to. Dr Peter Scott-Morgan, a man with a PhD in robotics and true British eccentric, was diagnosed with motor neurone disease in 2017. He spent the rest of his life turning himself into, in his own words, Peter 2.0, The Human Cyborg. With his irresistible charm, he rallied experts and collaborators including Fjord, Intel, and Rolls Royce to help bring his visionto life.

My role on the project was to prototype new accessibility capabilities and test them with Peter. I worked in an interdisciplinary team of designers, speech therapists, docters and hardware experts. We spent time living with Peter, learning how he used his computer, the tasks he needed to accomplish daily, and what communication meant to him. We spoke with his husband, carers, and family to get a deeper understanding of who he was.

I then worked closely with the Intel Labs team and implemented key new features into ACAT, the open-source accessibility tech suite originally built for Stephen Hawking.

  • Spherical Eye-Controlled Keyboard: We redesigned the standard QWERTY layout into a novel spherical keyboard, which reduced eye muscle strain. I then integrated an off-the-shelf gaming eye-controller into the ACAT platform using C# and custom Windows libraries.
  • Personality-Preserving Text To Speech: I iterated on the ACAT auto-complete tool, taking phrases from Peters's books and conversation recordings to hyper personalise the language in the auto complete, so he could access his unique wit and turns of phrase quickly.
  • Integrated 'Digital Essence': The main system peter interacted with was part of a larger system that combined our system with an AI voice voice and a hyper-realistic MetaHuman avatar, creating a fully responsive, eye-controlled interface that captured his true essence.

Reflecations in 2025: Peter predicted personalised LLMs!

This project took place in 2017 and i remember reading about early outputs from OpenAI's GPT. I am proud of what we achieved for Peter at the time, but of course this new dawn of LLMs means personality-preserving custom text generation has new truly arrived and can supercharge projects like this. It is testament to Peter's brilliant mind that even in 2017 he had essentially described a desire for a personalised AI agent, which has really come to reality mid way though the 2020s with Gemini, ChatGPT et al.

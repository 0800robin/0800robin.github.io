Peter 2.0 - Human Cyborg

Creating the next generation of assistive software interfaces for MND sufferers using eye-tracking.

Scott Morgan Foundation • Channel 4 Documentary • The Times Article • D&AD Future Impact Pencil

Peter 2.0 was, by some distance, the most extraordinary project I‚Äôve ever had the privilege to contribute to. Dr Peter Scott-Morgan, a man with a PhD in robotics and true British eccentric, was diagnosed with motor neurone disease in 2017. He spent the rest of his life turning himself into, in his own words, Peter 2.0, The Human Cyborg. With his irresistible charm, he rallied experts and collaborators including Fjord, Intel, and Rolls Royce to help bring his visionto life.

My role on the project was to work closely with the Intel Labs team to build out new features into ACAT, the open-source accessibility tech suite originally built for Stephen Hawking. We spent time living with Peter, learning how he used his computer, the tasks he needed to accomplish daily, and what communication meant to him. We spoke with his husband, carers, and family to get a deeper understanding of who he was.

I worked as part of an interdisciplinary team of speech therapists, doctors, UX designers, hardware experts and beyond. We redesigned the standard QWERTY keyboard with a spherical eye-controlled keyboard. This design reduced strain across the eye muscles and better suited his needs. I used an off-the-shelf gaming eye-controller and weaved this into ACAT with C# libraries and Windows.

Alongside this, we explored the retention of personality and spontaneity in digital communication, experimenting with early LLMs (large language models). This project took place just as ChatGPT was emerging.

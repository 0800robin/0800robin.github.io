ACAT

Assistive Context-Aware Toolkit (ACAT) is an open source platform enabling people with motor neuron diseases to have full access to the capabilities of their computers through very constrained interfaces.

In late 2019, Fjord and Intel partnered to design and develop a new, eye-tracking enabled interface for ACAT. We worked closely to test them with Peter Scott-Morgan, an English-American roboticist leveraging new technology in order to THRIVE with MND.A channel 4 documentary documenting Peters incredible journey is due to air mid-2020.

We designed and implemented a novel spherical keyboard design and a variety of useful new features based on our design research approach. I worked with a cross-disciplinary design and development team spread across the US and UK & my focus was ideating & implementing the new UI/UX designs. Peter is now using this version of ACAT as his way of communicating with the world.

ACAT 2.0 explores how eye-tracking can be used to give MND patients a faster way to communicate.

Assistive Context-Aware Toolkit (ACAT) is an open source platform enabling people with motor neuron diseases to have full access to the capabilities of their computers through very constrained interfaces. In late 2019, Fjord and Intel Labs partnered to design and develop a new, eye-tracking enabled interface for ACAT, an assistive technology system originally developed for Stephen Hawking.

We designed and implemented a novel spherical keyboard design and a variety of useful new features based on our design research approach. I worked with a cross-disciplinary design and development team spread across the US and UK & my focus was ideating & implementing the new UI/UX designs.

We tested ACAT 2.0 with Peter Scott-Morgan, an English-American roboticist leveraging new technology in order to THRIVE with MND. Peter is now using ACAT 2.0 as his primary way of communicating with the world and a channel 4 documentary documenting his incredible journey is due to air mid-2020

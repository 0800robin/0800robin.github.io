Project 1 - Burberry Animated Archive (2025)

My role in this project was Generative AI prototyping and the project started with us investigating how still imagery could be animated using AI. We explored a range of AI SAAS platforms, as well as OpenAI's 4o image generation, Google Veo, custom pipelines using ComfyUI and so on. We had many meeting with a range of vendors to gain insight into AI roadmaps and understand the state of the art. I then prototyped the same image/prompt across different platforms to appraise which was capable of creating an elevated enough output for a luxury brand.

A big challenge was navigating the legal implications of using generative AI on copyrighted material. We worked in close contact with the legal teams to pick the right tools and a framework that balanced innovation with brand protection.

The archive team selected three classic photographs by Lord Lichfield - quintessentially British scenes featuring a couple in the iconic Burberry trench coat. I led prompt engineering across a range of generative AI platforms and ultimately landed on Runway to animate subtle weather elements like fog and drifting clouds.

I found that using AI image segmentation to isolate backgrounds and animate only the atmospheric elements preserved the integrity of the original scenes. I collaborated with our in-house video team, providing them with the animated layers to perform the final compositing, who created a seamless, living portrait in post-production.

The final results are cinematic and together racked up over 1,000,000 views on Instagram reels.The CMO also showcased it during the global all-hands results day meeting shortly after posting. The result is a powerful juxtaposition: leveraging cutting-edge AI not to replace human creativity but to deepen the appreciation for the brand's timeless heritage and priceless aesthetic history.

Project 2 - 3D instore Archive microsite (2024)

This project began as an internal-facing 3D showcase that I built to celebrate the Thomas Burberry's 188th birthday.The tool was picked by the stores team to be adapated to become a premium story telling tool for the client advisors. The public facing version was adapated by a creative developer i manage and my role transitioned to code reviews, creative oversight and testing.

The experience was built in React Three Fibre and Drei, using a clean, declarative HTML-like structure to present the 3D content with clarity and polish. The core technical challenge remained ensuring a high-fidelity 3D experience was flawlessly performant on in-store tablets. The solution, which I had architected in the original version, involved a pipeline using meshopt for model optimisation and leveraging Google's model-viewer

The result was a future-facing tool that launched in 7 flagship stores worldwide, including Regent Street. The public version featured voice-note narrations from the Head of Archive, Carly Eck. The site established a scalable way for bringing Burberry‚Äôs 175 year archive to life in a weightless digital way, however far from home the store is.
